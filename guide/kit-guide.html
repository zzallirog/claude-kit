I · Nomen — what this is

The machine and the one who rules it

man — the oldest of guides; people have read it for half a century. This is an attempt at the same clarity, but in a human voice.

There is a laptop, and there is the person it belongs to. Between them stands a third — an assistant that can read the system, fix it, and advise. This guide describes not buttons but order: who may do what, where the assistant is free to act on its own, and where it must stop and ask.

It is written the way charters and old manuals are written: by section, from the general to the particular. You need not read it in order — it is enough to return to the chapter you need when you need it. The command kit-guide opens this page at any moment; the key Super + \ is even faster.

II · Personae — the cast

There are three of them

Each has his own measure of power and his own measure of responsibility. That is the whole design; the rest are consequences.

🜔
owner · the last word

The Owner

The one to whom the machine belongs. He holds the password and the right of the deciding "yes." The assistants do not enter the system on their own: only he carries the key. The request for a password is the human's signature under a deed.

🜍
architect · thinks

Opus

The senior mind. It plans the setup, holds the map — what lies where and what is fixed by what — advises and explains. This is the command post, not the hands.

🜎
operative · acts

Sonnet

Quick and light, it carries the routine. But it is let near the heart of the system more strictly: every dangerous step is a separate, one-time grant. The faster the runner, the shorter the leash.

III · Regna — two realms

Where it is free, and where — under guard

The border is simple to the point of severity: either it is plainly permitted, or the owner is plainly needed. There is no grey zone between them.

● home · ~/

The Free

  • read and write in your own folders
  • projects, notes, search
  • observe, measure, diagnose
Here the assistant moves without bowing — this is your home.
● system · root

Under Guard

  • updates and package installs
  • firewall, ports, services
  • drivers, time, network
Here — only by rite: request → your grant → your password → stand down.
IV · Ritus — commands

Words that change something

Each entry opens at a touch. The button drops the word into the clipboard — all that remains is to utter it in the terminal.

— power over the system —
update update the world and inspect the walls — in one word root ▸
Synchronizes and installs updates with the native package manager — pacman, apt or dnf, whichever is found. Then it makes its rounds: is the firewall lit, have any services fallen, is a reboot due, which ports face outward. System actions always ask first. update --dry-run — walk the path in words, touching nothing.
fix healer: disk, names, network, packages, services root ▸
It works through a list of common ailments. The safe it cures on its own, the risky — only after asking. Whatever it cannot overcome it records, to later get at the root and add to the book of maladies. fix --auto — in one pass; fix --dry-run — only show.
— memory and the way —
where the map: what lies where on this land safe ▸
Unfolds the owner's map: where the projects are, where the notes, where the logs, which words are available. It lives in ~/.config/kit-claude/layout.md — rewrite it freely.
recall "…" recall: across notes and the system's chronicle at once safe ▸
A single door into "what I know" and "what happened": it searches the knowledge base ~/kb and the system journal. recall --notes — notes only, recall --logs — the chronicle only.
— help and light —
kit-help a short scroll in the terminal (also shown on login) safe ▸
A short list of words right in the window; it also greets you on opening (via fastfetch), so you need keep nothing in your head.
kit-guide open this codex in the browser safe ▸
Opens the page you are reading. Bound to Super + \ — see chapter VII.
claude call upon the assistant — in your own language AI ▸
Summons the assistant in the current folder. If it is unclear what a command does or how to set it up — ask in words.
V · Mos — the order

Order on the outside — freedom within

"Disk is cheap, the head is dear" — and so here things are kept with a margin, not cut to the bone.

The idea of the order is simple: push the structure outward — into folders, notes, the map — so the head stays free for thought. Do not optimize prematurely. Do not deduplicate. Leave traces.

Projects live in separate courtyards — ~/projects/name/, each on its own. Need one file in two places — let it lie as a copy in both. Two honest copies beat one clever link you will forget about.

The knowledge base — ~/kb/, plain notes. Append the new at the bottom, with a date; do not erase the old. To search — the word recall. The default shell installed is fish: it suggests and completes for you.

What the assistant does especially well

These skills awaken on their own for a task — or by name, through /name.

VIII · Appendices — appendices

The full charters

What the chapters say briefly is here in full. These same texts sit on disk alongside the kit.

App. AThe Charter of the Grant

The root of power belongs to the human. On a newcomer's machine the cost of a mistake is high, and so root is never "poured out" across the scripts. The only door into the system is the rite, and it is always passed in four beats:

┌─ GRANT REQUEST ─ root ──────────────────────────── │ requested by : AI operative │ deed : close the stray port 5355 │ incantation : sudo ufw deny 5355 │ risk : 🟡 medium │ ONLY the owner may permit. The assistant will not enter alone. └─────────────────────────────────────────────────── Owner, grant access? [y/N]: y ● grant given. the system asks for your password — this is the signature: [password entry — here the human decides] ✓ CONFIRMED — done. temporary root revoked. stand down.

"Temporary root" is literal: the assistant borrows it for one deed and returns it at once. Permanent power over the system it never has. The password is yours and yours alone; it is the very proof that the human decided.

App. BThe Way of Work

Side projects — in courtyards (~/projects/name/), no clutter and no deduplication. Knowledge base — ~/kb/, append at the bottom with a date, keep the old. Map — where, search — recall, shell — fish. The spirit is simple: keep your traces, do not cut to the bone, trust your future self.

App. COf Root and the Guard

What the kit does not do: it does not disable the sudo password, does not hand out passwordless root, does not edit /etc/sudoers, does not open ports on its own. Freedom is in the home (~/); the guard is at the threshold of the system: writing to /etc, /usr, /boot and anything destructive are sealed shut.

You decide · Opus thinks · Sonnet acts · the key is yours.
