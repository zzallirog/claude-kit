# START HERE 👋

This is your starter kit for Claude Code. It's been put together in advance so you
don't have to set everything up from scratch.

## What to do (3 steps)

1. **Unpack** this archive wherever is convenient, e.g. into your home folder:
   ```
   ~/kit-claude/
   ```

2. **Open Claude Code** in this folder:
   ```
   cd ~/kit-claude
   claude
   ```

3. **Tell Claude one phrase:**
   > read BOOTSTRAP.md and begin

From there Claude will, on its own:
- ask you a short intake survey (what you need from the computer and from the AI);
- check your computer (which processor, graphics card, operating system);
- tell you which "skills" (ready-made helper abilities) you have and what
  you can do with each;
- offer to install the ones you need, one by one — **it installs nothing without your "yes"**;
- set up convenient commands and explain what each one does;
- offer a safe configuration and speed-up of the system for your hardware.

## Commands that will appear (after installation)
- **`update`** — update the system and run a security check in one command.
- **`fix`** — fix common problems: disk space, DNS, Wi-Fi, packages,
  services. `fix --auto` does the safe stuff itself and asks about the risky stuff.
- **`where`** — a map of the laptop: what lives where.
- **`recall "something"`** — search your notes and system logs.
- **`kit-help`** — a cheatsheet of the commands (the same one is shown when you open
  the terminal, so you don't have to memorize it).
- **`kit-guide`** (or the key **Super+\\**) — a nice dossier in the browser:
  blocks of what exists and what it does. Click a command — it's copied, try it.

## Who's in charge here (read `THE-PROTOCOL.md` — 5 minutes)
- **You** are the owner: the password and the final word are yours.
- **Opus** thinks (system configuration), **Sonnet** does the routine.
- Home (`~/`) — free rein. The system (root: ports, firewall, updates) — only
  with your grant and your password. These commands **never do anything dangerous silently**.

## Important about the model

The kit is designed for the **Opus** model (it's smarter at planning and configuration).
If Sonnet is on right now — Claude will tell you so itself at the start.
To switch: type the Claude command
```
/model
```
and choose Opus. If Opus isn't available on your plan — Sonnet can handle it too,
just a bit more simply. Claude will continue either way.

## If something is unclear

Just ask Claude in your own words — it answers in your language and explains
things in plain terms. You're a beginner, that's fine: the kit is made exactly for a beginner.
